About Aurora

Aurora Cramer (they/she) is currently a PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering at New York University, under the direction of Juan Pablo Bello. research focuses on incorporating natural structure into transfer learning frameworks to develop more effective machine listening models without large annotated datasets, using hierarchical relationships, task relationships, multi-modal dynamics, and embodied navigation. is joining Zoox as a Machine Learning and Signal Processing Engineer.

Aurora obtained B.S. in Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences from U.C. Berkeley in 2015, where completed the EECS Honors program with a focus in Music/Audio. In 2017, Aurora started graduate studies in Electrical and Computer Engineering in the Masters program at New York University as a Samuel Morse Fellow, before transferring to the PhD program in 2018. involved in the NSF-funded SONYC and BirdVox projects, both of which have been successfully completed.

Aurora has worked in the space of audio and machine learning in industry. Before joining Zoox, an intern at Mitsubishi Electric Research Laboratories during the Fall of 2020 working on environment source separation. Previously, an intern on the Applied Deep Learning Research team at NVIDIA during the summer of 2018 working on audio inpainting. a research engineer on the Applied Research team at Gracenote from 2015-2017 where developed audio classification systems for music. In 2014, a media engineering intern at BlueJeans Network, where worked on refactoring, testing, and improving noise suppression systems for video conferencing platforms.
